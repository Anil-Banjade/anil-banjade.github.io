Anil Banjade

Bachelor’s in Computer Engineering  ·  Institute of Engineering, Thapathali Campus, Nepal

I am a fourth-year Computer Engineering student at Thapathali Campus, Nepal, with a strong focus on computer vision, machine learning, and 3D scene understanding. My work centers on bridging classical geometric methods with modern deep learning approaches to build systems that can perceive and reconstruct the real world from visual data.

Currently, I am developing a depth-supervised NeRF-SLAM pipeline that integrates visual feature extraction, pose estimation, loop closure, and neural rendering for photorealistic 3D reconstruction from monocular video. Alongside this, I have built and trained deep learning models including U-Net for image segmentation (achieving high Dice and IoU scores), GAN-based image synthesis models, and a Nepali multimodal system aligning text and vision embeddings. My interests lie in solving real-world perception problems at the intersection of geometry, learning, and representation.

Projects

2025 — Present

Bridging Classical Geometric SLAM and Neural Rendering via Depth-Supervised NeRF

Developing a unified framework that combines geometric SLAM with Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) for photorealistic 3D scene reconstruction from monocular video. The pipeline integrates visual feature extraction, pose estimation, loop closure detection, and bundle adjustment with a depth-supervised NeRF module — improving geometric consistency and accelerating convergence. Targets applications in robotics, AR/VR, and autonomous navigation.

Computer Vision Neural Rendering 3D Reconstruction SLAM PyTorch
2025

U-Net Based Medical Image Segmentation

Implemented U-Net from scratch in PyTorch with encoder–decoder architecture and skip connections. Applied to polyp segmentation on the Kvasir endoscopic dataset and vehicle segmentation on Carvana, achieving a Dice score of 0.971 and IoU of 0.948 — demonstrating strong generalization to both medical and real-world visual domains.

Medical Imaging Segmentation Deep Learning PyTorch

2025

Generative Adversarial Networks for Image Synthesis

Trained GAN and DCGAN architectures to synthesize 128×128 images across MNIST, CelebA, and a custom classroom dataset. Designed convolutional generator and discriminator networks, monitored training with FID scores, and investigated failure modes including mode collapse, aliasing artifacts, and dataset-size limitations.

2024

Nepali Multimodal Large Language Model

Aligned NepBERTa text embeddings and ResNet-50 vision embeddings through projection layers and contrastive learning to build a shared cross-modal embedding space. Trained a transformer-based autoregressive decoder to generate Nepali text conditioned on multimodal features — laying groundwork for low-resource multimodal AI in Nepali.
